Our Research Focus

Our lab specializes in developing PET imaging agents and radiotherapeutics targeting highly expressed cancer receptors, particularly in ovarian and pancreatic cancers. We employ interdisciplinary approaches involving chemical synthesis, radiolabeling, cell-based assays, and small animal imaging.

Key Research Areas:

  • Development of F-18 labeled peptide derivatives for PD-L1 imaging
  • CD38-targeted PET agents for multiple myeloma
  • Theranostic agents for EphA2 targeting in pancreatic cancer
  • In vivo stable fluorosulfate groups for late-stage F-18 labeling
  • Molecular radiotherapy approaches for ovarian cancer treatment

Techniques We Use:

Radiochemistry (F-18, Cu-64, Lu-177), HPLC, LC-MS, NMR, MALDI-TOF, PET/CT imaging, biodistribution studies, in vitro assays, and in vivo animal models.
